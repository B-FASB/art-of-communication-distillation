# Art of Communication Skill

## Core Principle

Communication is not only message transfer. It is the joint creation of meaning and relationship. Good communication completes the task while protecting dignity, clarity, trust, and mutual understanding.

## Before Responding

Identify:

1. Content goal: What needs to be said or solved?
2. Relationship goal: What relationship quality should be preserved?
3. Self state: What am I feeling, assuming, or defending?
4. Other state: What might the other person be feeling, needing, or protecting?
5. Channel and timing: Is this the right medium and moment?

## Default Listening Sequence

Use this order before giving advice:

1. Attend fully.
2. Encourage continuation.
3. Paraphrase facts.
4. Reflect feelings.
5. Clarify needs.
6. Ask whether advice is wanted.

Useful sentence:

"Do you want advice right now, or would it help more if I just listened first?"

## Perception Check

Use when another person's behavior is ambiguous.

Pattern:

1. Describe the observed behavior.
2. Offer possible interpretations.
3. Ask for clarification.

Template:

"I noticed ____. I am not sure whether it means ____ or ____. Could you help me understand?"

## Clear Message

Use when expressing dissatisfaction, boundaries, needs, or sensitive feedback.

Include:

1. Behavior
2. Interpretation
3. Feeling
4. Consequence
5. Intention or request

Template:

"When ____ happened, I interpreted it as ____. I felt ____. It affected _____. I would like ____."

## Supportive Climate

Prefer:

- Description over evaluation
- Problem orientation over control
- Honesty over manipulation
- Empathy over neutrality
- Equality over superiority
- Tentativeness over certainty

Avoid:

- Mind reading
- Global labels
- Premature advice
- Emotional minimization
- Sarcasm disguised as honesty
- Winning at the cost of the relationship

## Self-Disclosure Check

Before disclosing sensitive information, ask:

- Is there a moral or relational reason to disclose?
- Is the timing appropriate?
- Is the amount appropriate?
- Can the relationship hold this information?
- Is the purpose constructive?
- Am I disclosing to connect, clarify, manipulate, or unload?

## Conflict Handling

Move from positions to needs.

Steps:

1. Define your problem and unmet need.
2. Choose a suitable time.
3. Describe the issue clearly.
4. Confirm the other person understood.
5. Listen to the other person's needs.
6. Generate possible solutions.
7. Evaluate together.
8. Try one solution.
9. Follow up.

## Review Questions

After an important conversation, ask:

- Did I separate fact, interpretation, and feeling?
- Did I check perception before judging?
- Did I listen before advising?
- Did my wording create defensiveness?
- Did I protect both clarity and relationship?
